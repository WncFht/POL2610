## c01. Why Do Japan and South Korea Want Both?

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Hook the audience with the puzzle and prevent them from reading the talk as simple side-choosing.
- Open: "The puzzle is simple: if Japan and South Korea are moving closer to the United States, why are they still keeping channels with China?"
- Image cue: Briefly point to the Camp David photo and the CJK Summit photo as the two visual anchors of the entire talk.
- Beats:
1. The coexistence of the two tracks looks contradictory at first glance.
2. My argument is that it is a problem of alliance management, not indecision.
3. The whole presentation will explain how two risks are being managed at once.
- Transition: "To see why this matters, we first need a quick visual overview of what changed in the region after 2023."
- Time: 50s

**English**

Today I want to discuss a puzzle in Northeast Asia. If Japan and South Korea are strengthening security cooperation with the United States, why are they still trying to preserve institutional channels with China? At first glance, this looks contradictory. It can even look like indecision. The two images on the cover already capture that tension: Camp David represents a harder security track, while the CJK summit image reminds us that diplomatic channels did not disappear. But I will argue that this is neither contradiction nor indecision. My main claim is that Japan and South Korea are not simply choosing between Washington and Beijing. Instead, they are managing two different strategic risks at the same time. They want stronger U.S. security insurance, but they also want to avoid being trapped in a fully closed confrontation with China. So the key to understanding recent Northeast Asian politics is not simple side-choosing. It is alliance management.

**中文**

今天我想讨论东北亚中的一个核心谜题：如果日本和韩国正在加强与美国的安全合作，那它们为什么又仍然努力保留与中国的制度性沟通渠道？乍一看，这似乎是矛盾的，甚至像是一种摇摆不定。封面上的两张图其实已经把这种张力表现出来了：Camp David 代表更硬的安全轨道，而中日韩峰会则提醒我们，面向中国的外交渠道并没有消失。但我的论点是，这既不是矛盾，也不是犹豫。日本和韩国并不是在华盛顿和北京之间做一次性的选择，而是在同时管理两类不同的战略风险。它们一方面需要更强的美国安全保险，另一方面又不希望自己被锁死在与中国全面对抗的轨道中。所以，理解当前东北亚政治的关键，不是“选边站”，而是“联盟管理”。

## c02. One Region, Two Tracks

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Establish the empirical base before theory.
- Open: "From 2023 to 2025, Northeast Asia did not move in one direction. It moved on two tracks at the same time."
- Image cue: Use the two photos as concrete proof-points before listing the milestones.
- Beats:
1. Track one is the U.S.-Japan-ROK security institutionalization track.
2. Track two is the revived CJK and China-facing interface track.
3. The coexistence of these tracks is the empirical fact that the rest of the talk explains.
- Transition: "Once we see the two tracks, the next question is how to explain them without falling back on vague words like balancing or indecision."
- Time: 1m20s

**English**

The reason this question matters is that from 2023 to 2025, Northeast Asia developed along two tracks at the same time. On this slide, the Camp David meeting photo on the left shows the security side becoming more procedural and organized, while the CJK summit photo on the right shows that diplomatic interface-building was also revived. On the one hand, there was a clear movement toward deeper U.S.-Japan-ROK institutionalization. The major milestones include the Washington Declaration in April 2023, Camp David in August 2023, the 2024 trilateral defense ministerial statement, the U.S.-Japan extended deterrence statement, and the fourth U.S.-ROK Nuclear Consultative Group meeting in January 2025. On the other hand, the China-Japan-ROK track also revived. The ninth trilateral summit in May 2024 restarted cooperation, and the eleventh trilateral foreign ministers’ meeting in March 2025 showed that this track was still functioning. Japan also resumed the high-level economic dialogue with China, and South Korea held a foreign ministers’ meeting with China. So the region did not move in one single direction. It moved into a dual-track structure.

**中文**

这个问题之所以重要，是因为从 2023 年到 2025 年，东北亚实际上是沿着两条轨道同时发展的。这一页上的两张照片本身就很说明问题：左边的 Camp David 会场图，代表安全合作越来越程序化、制度化；右边的中日韩峰会图，则说明面向中国的外交接口也在恢复。一方面，美日韩安全合作明显走向了更深的制度化。关键节点包括 2023 年 4 月的《华盛顿宣言》、2023 年 8 月的 Camp David 文件、2024 年的三边防长声明、美日延伸威慑部长级声明，以及 2025 年 1 月的第四次美韩 NCG。另一方面，中日韩轨道也在恢复。2024 年 5 月的第九次中日韩峰会重启了合作，2025 年 3 月的第十一次中日韩外长会则表明这条轨道并没有中断。与此同时，日本恢复了对华高层经济对话，韩国也举行了韩中外长会。因此，东北亚并不是朝着单一方向前进，而是进入了“双轨并行”的结构。

## c03. Key Idea: Alliance Management, Not Simple Side-Choosing

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Introduce the main theoretical frame in the lightest possible way.
- Open: "I explain this through alliance theory, especially the alliance dilemma."
- Image cue: Use the three book covers as a visual reminder that the argument is grounded in a literature, not invented ad hoc.
- Beats:
1. Define abandonment in plain language.
2. Define entrapment in plain language.
3. Link U.S. reassurance to abandonment reduction.
4. Link China interface preservation to entrapment and instability reduction.
- Transition: "With that theory in mind, let me turn to the first country case: Japan."
- Time: 1m20s

**English**

To explain this, I use alliance theory, especially the idea of the alliance dilemma. The book covers on the left are just a visual reminder that this argument stands on an existing literature about alliance politics, extended deterrence, and middle-power strategy. In simple terms, allies usually worry about two opposite dangers. The first is abandonment: the fear that the ally, in this case the United States, may not be reliable enough in a crisis. The second is entrapment: the fear that the ally may pull you into conflicts or costs you do not fully want. For Japan and South Korea, stronger deterrence cooperation with the United States helps reduce abandonment risk. But keeping some institutional interface with China helps reduce entrapment, economic retaliation, and crisis escalation. This is why I prefer the term alliance management. It is more precise than saying Japan and South Korea are just hedging, and it is also more precise than saying they have already made a complete choice.

**中文**

为了解释这一点，我采用联盟理论，尤其是“联盟困境”这个概念。左边这三本书的封面，可以把它们看成一个视觉提示：我的论证并不是临时拼出来的，而是建立在联盟政治、延伸威慑和中等国家战略这些既有文献之上。简单来说，盟友通常同时担心两种相反的风险。第一种是 abandonment，也就是“被抛弃”的风险：在危机中，美国是否真的足够可靠。第二种是 entrapment，也就是“被拖累”的风险：盟友是否会把你卷入你并不完全愿意承担的冲突和代价。对于日本和韩国来说，加强与美国的威慑合作，有助于降低 abandonment 风险；而保留与中国的某些制度接口，则有助于降低 entrapment、经济报复和危机升级的风险。所以我更愿意使用“联盟管理”这个概念。它比简单说日韩在 hedging 更准确，也比说它们已经彻底选边更准确。

## c04. Japan Case 1: Why Tokyo Wanted Harder U.S. Insurance

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Show the security-insurance side of the Japanese case.
- Open: "Japan first looks like a very clear case of stronger alliance reassurance."
- Image cue: Use the Yokota photo to stress that reassurance was enacted through repeated trilateral military coordination.
- Beats:
1. Security conditions looked worse to Tokyo.
2. Camp David made trilateral cooperation more regular and less ad hoc.
3. U.S.-Japan extended deterrence documents made reassurance more formal and visible.
4. Tie the whole slide back to abandonment reduction.
- Transition: "But the Japan case only makes sense if we also ask why Tokyo still left some room for China-facing channels."
- Time: 1m25s

**English**

Let me start with Japan. Why did Tokyo want stronger U.S. insurance? The short answer is that Japan saw the regional security environment as getting worse, while it also wanted U.S. commitments to become more institutional and predictable. The Yokota photo on this slide matters because it shows that reassurance was not only written into documents. It was also performed through repeated trilateral military coordination. Camp David mattered because it turned trilateral cooperation into something more regular: annual meetings, annual exercises, more structured coordination, and faster consultation. The U.S.-Japan statement on extended deterrence and the later Guidelines for Extended Deterrence mattered for a similar reason. They moved the alliance from vague reassurance to more formalized consultation and planning. In alliance-theory terms, Japan was trying to reduce abandonment risk. It was not enough to trust the alliance in general. Tokyo wanted procedures, meetings, and documents that could make American commitments more visible and more durable.

**中文**

先看日本。东京为什么想要更强的美国安全保险？简短的回答是，日本判断地区安全环境在恶化，同时它也希望美国的承诺变得更加制度化、更加可预测。这一页上的横田基地照片很重要，因为它提醒我们，reassurance 不只是写进文件里，也通过反复出现的三边军事协调被“演出来”“做出来”。Camp David 之所以重要，是因为它把三边合作推进成一种更常态的安排：年度会议、年度演习、更结构化的协调以及更快的磋商。美日延伸威慑部长级声明，以及后来的《延伸威慑指针》，之所以重要，也是因为它们把同盟从比较模糊的 reassurance，推进到了更正式的咨询与规划层面。用联盟理论的话说，日本是在努力降低 abandonment 风险。仅仅“相信同盟”已经不够了，东京希望看到程序、会议和文件，把美国的承诺变得更可见、更持久。

## c05. Japan Case 2: Why Tokyo Still Kept China Channels Open

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Prevent the audience from reducing Japan to pure containment.
- Open: "But Japan is not just a story of harder balancing."
- Image cue: Clarify that the book cover is a visual shorthand for a constrained new phase in China-Japan relations, not the evidence itself.
- Beats:
1. Introduce the defense hotline as crisis-management evidence.
2. Introduce the high-level economic dialogue as controlled interface evidence.
3. Clarify that this is not trust, but a limited interface.
4. Summarize Japan as balancing-heavy, not interface-free.
- Transition: "South Korea faces the same dilemma, but the balance is different."
- Time: 1m15s

**English**

But the Japanese case is not just about balancing. Even as Tokyo strengthened deterrence, it also kept some China-facing channels open. The image on this slide is a visual shorthand for that point: China-Japan ties entered a more constrained and carefully managed phase, but they did not disappear. A good example is the hotline between the Japanese and Chinese defense authorities, established in March 2023. Another is the sixth Japan-China High-Level Economic Dialogue in March 2025, which discussed practical cooperation, business conditions, green economy, and communication on export controls and critical minerals. These do not mean Japan suddenly trusted China. They mean Tokyo still saw value in crisis management and controlled economic engagement. So Japan’s strategy was not pure containment. It was stronger balancing combined with narrow but useful interfaces.

**中文**

但日本这个 case 并不只是“平衡中国”这么简单。即使东京在加强威慑，它也仍然保留了一些面向中国的渠道。这一页上的书封可以把它理解成一个视觉提示：中日关系进入了一个更受限、但并未断裂的新阶段。一个典型例子是 2023 年 3 月建立的日中防务热线。另一个例子是 2025 年 3 月恢复的第六次中日高层经济对话，其中讨论了务实合作、营商环境、绿色经济，以及出口管制和关键矿产方面的沟通。这并不意味着日本突然信任中国，而是说明东京仍然认为，危机管控和有限度的经济接触是有价值的。因此，日本的策略并不是纯粹的遏制，而是“更强的平衡”加上“有限但有用的接口”。

## c06. South Korea Case 1: Why Seoul Wanted Even More Reassurance

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Show that the Korean case is a stronger reassurance case than Japan.
- Open: "If Japan wanted more reassurance, South Korea wanted even more."
- Image cue: Use the missile-defense exercise photo to emphasize the operational visibility of reassurance.
- Beats:
1. Washington Declaration created a sharper reassurance upgrade.
2. NCG matters because it turned reassurance into a continuing process.
3. Peninsula nuclear anxiety makes U.S. credibility more immediate in South Korea.
4. Conclude that Seoul pushed harder for visible reassurance.
- Transition: "But stronger reassurance did not mean South Korea was willing to erase China from its diplomatic equation."
- Time: 1m25s

**English**

Now let us turn to South Korea. In some ways, the Korean case is even more revealing, because Seoul’s need for reassurance was more immediate and more visible. The exercise image on this slide is useful because it shows that reassurance was not only diplomatic language. It also had an operational and public-facing side. The Washington Declaration in April 2023 is crucial here. It created the Nuclear Consultative Group, expanded nuclear and strategic planning, strengthened consultations, and increased the visibility of U.S. strategic assets. The fourth NCG statement in January 2025 then showed that this was not symbolic. It had become a continuing process involving planning, exercises, communications systems, and coordination between nuclear and conventional dimensions. Why did South Korea want this so badly? Because on the peninsula, the nuclear problem is immediate, public anxiety is stronger, and the credibility of U.S. extended deterrence matters in a much more direct way. So Seoul pushed harder for visible reassurance.

**中文**

再来看韩国。在某种意义上，韩国这个 case 更能说明问题，因为首尔对 reassurance 的需求更直接，也更可见。这一页上的导弹防御演训照片很有帮助，因为它告诉我们，reassurance 不只是外交话语，也有操作层面和公众可见层面。这里最关键的是 2023 年 4 月的《华盛顿宣言》。它建立了 NCG，扩大了核与战略规划，加强了磋商，并提高了美国战略资产可见性。到 2025 年 1 月的第四次 NCG 联合声明时，我们已经能看出这不只是象征动作，而是一个持续运行的流程，涉及规划、演训、通信系统以及核与常规层面的协调。韩国为什么如此迫切地需要这些安排？因为在半岛上，核问题是立刻存在的，公众焦虑也更强，美国延伸威慑的可信度对韩国来说更加直接、更加现实。因此，首尔比东京更积极地推动“可见的 reassurance”。

## c07. South Korea Case 2: Why Seoul Also Kept a China Interface

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Explain why South Korea keeps a thicker China interface than Japan.
- Open: "South Korea did not abandon its China interface, because its strategic environment made that too costly."
- Image cue: Use the book cover as a visual cue for the peninsula context behind Seoul's China policy.
- Beats:
1. China matters to South Korea beyond trade because of the Korean Peninsula.
2. The foreign ministers' meeting shows the interface was preserved, not abandoned.
3. This is not anti-alliance behavior; it is alliance management under tighter constraints.
4. Summarize South Korea as interface-heavy.
- Transition: "Now we can compare the two countries directly."
- Time: 1m15s

**English**

At the same time, South Korea also kept a stronger China interface than Japan. The reason is not difficult to see. The image on this slide is useful because it points us back to the peninsula context: China matters to South Korea not only economically, but also strategically around North Korea and regional stability. Beijing remains relevant to North Korea, to regional stability, and to the wider diplomatic environment around the peninsula. That is why the Korea-China foreign ministers’ meeting in May 2024 is important. Seoul emphasized mutual respect, reciprocity, shared interests, and the need for more strategic communication. This does not mean South Korea rejected the alliance path. It means Seoul did not want its stronger alliance strategy to eliminate all room for China-related diplomacy. In other words, South Korea sought stronger insurance from the United States, but it also worked to avoid over-locking itself into a single confrontation track.

**中文**

与此同时，韩国保留的对华接口实际上比日本更厚一些。原因并不难理解。这一页上的书封提示我们要把视角放回半岛：中国对韩国的重要性不仅体现在经济上，更体现在朝鲜半岛本身。北京仍然与朝鲜问题、地区稳定以及半岛周边外交环境密切相关。这就是为什么 2024 年 5 月的韩中外长会很重要。韩方在会谈中强调 mutual respect、reciprocity、shared interests，以及加强战略沟通的必要性。这并不意味着韩国拒绝同盟路线，而是说明首尔并不希望自己在强化同盟之后，彻底失去与中国有关的外交空间。换句话说，韩国一方面向美国索取更强的安全保险，另一方面也努力避免自己被锁死在单一对抗轨道中。

## c08. Japan and South Korea: Same Dilemma, Different Balance

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Lock in the comparison and make the two-case distinction memorable.
- Open: "The logic is the same in both cases, but the weighting is not."
- Beats:
1. Japan is more balancing-heavy.
2. South Korea is more interface-heavy.
3. Both still fit the same alliance-management framework.
4. Stress that difference in weight is not difference in underlying logic.
- Transition: "Once we compare them, we can scale the argument up from state strategy to regional order."
- Time: 1m10s

**English**

So if we compare the two cases, the basic logic is the same but the weighting is different. Japan is more balancing-heavy. It strengthens the alliance very clearly, and its China interface is narrower, focused mainly on crisis management and economic governance. South Korea is more interface-heavy. It also deepens alliance reassurance, but it has even stronger reasons to keep communication with China alive, because of North Korea and because of its higher sensitivity to economic and diplomatic fallout. So I would not say Japan and South Korea follow identical strategies. I would say they operate under the same alliance-management logic, but they assign different weights to the two sides of that logic.

**中文**

因此，把日本和韩国放在一起比较时，我们会发现：两者的基本逻辑相同，但权重不同。日本更偏向 balancing-heavy。它非常明确地强化同盟，而它对华接口更窄，主要集中在危机管控和经济治理。韩国则更偏向 interface-heavy。它同样加深了同盟 reassurance，但由于朝鲜问题以及对经济和外交后果的更高敏感性，它有更强的理由保留与中国的沟通。因此，我不会说日本和韩国采取了完全一样的策略，而会说它们处在同一个 alliance-management 逻辑之下，只是对这一逻辑两端赋予了不同权重。

## c09. What This Says About Northeast Asia

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Convert two country cases into a regional-order conclusion.
- Open: "If we put the two cases together, we can see a broader regional logic."
- Image cue: Point to the two photos as shorthand for two real and simultaneous institutional lanes.
- Beats:
1. Hard security is becoming more structured through U.S.-Japan-ROK.
2. Buffering, diplomacy, and governance still require China-facing interfaces.
3. Therefore the regional order is bifurcated by function, not fully split by membership.
4. This is why dual-trilateralism is analytically useful.
- Transition: "One final check is whether this pattern survived beyond the main 2023-2025 formation period."
- Time: 1m20s

**English**

Once we put the two cases together, we can see a broader regional pattern. The two photos on this slide help make the point visually: Camp David stands for the hard-security lane, while the CJK summit stands for the continued interface lane. Northeast Asia is being reorganized by function. Hard security is increasingly structured through U.S.-Japan-ROK deterrence mechanisms. But buffering, diplomacy, and some forms of economic governance still require China-facing interfaces. This is why the region should not be described too simply as a story of total decoupling or complete bloc formation. It is more accurate to say that the order is becoming bifurcated by function. Security cooperation is hardening, but diplomatic and economic interfaces have not disappeared. In fact, they remain necessary precisely because security competition is becoming sharper.

**中文**

当我们把两个 case 放在一起时，就能看到一个更大的地区图景。这一页上的两张照片也在帮助你把这个逻辑视觉化：Camp David 代表硬安全轨道，中日韩峰会代表接口轨道，而且这两条轨道是真实并存的。东北亚秩序正在按照“功能”被重新组织。硬安全越来越通过美日韩威慑机制来构建；但缓冲、外交以及某些经济治理功能，仍然需要面向中国的制度接口。这也就是为什么我们不能把这个地区简单描述成“全面脱钩”或者“彻底集团化”。更准确的说法是：地区秩序正在按功能发生分层。安全合作在变硬，但外交与经济接口并没有消失。恰恰因为安全竞争在加剧，这些接口反而仍然是必要的。

## c10. Did the Pattern Stop in 2025?

- Open: "A reasonable challenge is whether this pattern ended in 2025. It did not."
- Beats:
1. The U.S.-Japan-ROK track continued to deepen.
2. The CJK track moved more slowly but did not disappear.
3. The post-2025 pattern supports the original argument rather than overturning it.
- Transition: "So let me end with the shortest version of the argument."
- Time: 50s

**English**

A final question is whether this pattern stopped in 2025. The answer is no. The later evidence suggests that the U.S.-Japan-ROK side continued to deepen through later meetings and the new trilateral coordinating machinery, while the China-Japan-ROK side remained slower but still alive. So the dual-track logic was not just a short-lived moment. It continued beyond the main formation period, even if the two tracks did not move at the same speed.

**中文**

最后一个问题是，这种模式在 2025 年之后有没有停止。答案是否定的。后续证据显示，美日韩一侧通过后续会议以及新的三边协调机制继续深化；而中日韩一侧虽然节奏更慢，但依然在运行。因此，双轨逻辑并不是一个短暂的瞬间，而是在主要形成期之后仍然延续着，只不过两条轨道的推进速度并不相同。

## c11. Final Takeaway

**Presenter Notes**

- Purpose: Leave the audience with one sentence they can remember and repeat.
- Open: "My conclusion is simple."
- Beats:
1. Japan and South Korea are not simply choosing one side.
2. They use U.S. security insurance to reduce abandonment.
3. They keep China-facing interfaces to reduce entrapment and instability.
4. This is the clearest sign of a new bifurcated but connected Northeast Asian order.
- Transition: "End."
- Time: 50s

**English**

So my conclusion is this: Japan and South Korea are not simply choosing America over China or China over America. They are managing two different strategic risks through two different institutional tracks. They strengthen U.S. security insurance to reduce abandonment, and they preserve China-facing interfaces to reduce entrapment and instability. If we see the region in this way, then the parallel rise of U.S.-Japan-ROK and China-Japan-ROK is not a contradiction. It is the clearest sign that Northeast Asia is entering a new, bifurcated, but still connected regional order.

**中文**

所以我的结论是：日本和韩国并不是在简单地“选择美国”或者“选择中国”。它们是在通过两套不同的制度轨道，管理两种不同的战略风险。它们加强美国安全保险，是为了降低 abandonment；它们保留面向中国的接口，是为了降低 entrapment 和不稳定性。如果我们用这种方式来理解这个地区，那么美日韩与中日韩的并行上升就不是一种矛盾，而是东北亚正在进入一种新的、按功能分层、但又并未完全断裂的地区秩序的最清晰表现。
